# Build interview

<!-- Ask in rounds of 2-3, not all at once. The structure is in how they describe
     their week; your job is to surface it, not to impose one. -->

## Round 1 - the shape of the business

- What does the agency sell? (services, not the pitch)
- How many active clients right now, and is that growing?
- Who works in this workspace - just you, or a team? Who else needs to find things without asking you?

## Round 2 - the unit of work

- Walk me through one client, one normal week. What actually happens?
- What is the repeating deliverable? (a report, a campaign, a sprint, a retainer month)
- Where do you stop and check something before it goes out? (these become human gates)

## Round 3 - what is stable vs. what changes

- What stays the same across every client? (methodology, quality bar, tools) -> departments
- What is different per client? (brand, goals, quirks, state) -> client folders
- Is there anything you keep re-explaining to new team members? -> that is a file, not a conversation

## Round 4 - scale check

- Under 5 clients or solo: recommend the minimal build (client triad only, no departments split, no workflows). Say why: the full skeleton earns its weight at 8+ clients or 3+ service lines.
- Existing folder structure already? -> switch to restructure mode, audit first.
- Do they use Claude Code specifically? -> enforcement layer is available later, note it, do not build it now.
